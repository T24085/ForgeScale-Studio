Commission Levels

Choose the type of finish you need

Final pricing depends on scale, prep work, model complexity, effects, and quantity, but these tiers make it easy to scope the request.

Tabletop Ready

Clean, cohesive tabletop color work for campaigns, skirmish forces, and game-night pieces.

  • Strong readability at play distance
  • Selective highlights and basing
  • Best for squads and recurring unit types

Showcase

Higher-contrast character work with more layering, cleaner transitions, and attention on focal areas.

  • Character miniatures and boss models
  • Palette matching from references
  • Optional weathering and effects notes

Display Piece

Built for shelves, gifts, and photo-worthy centerpieces where the finish matters as much as the sculpt.

  • Busts, statues, or premium hero models
  • More time spent on texture and mood
  • Best for one-off commissions

Print + Paint Bundle

For clients who need the whole pipeline handled from production through final painted delivery.

  • ForgeScale printing and cleanup
  • Artist handoff for painting
  • One combined project review

Best Fit

What helps a paint commission move faster

Clear references

Character art, screenshots, mood boards, prior paint jobs, and palette notes reduce guesswork immediately.

Accurate model details

Scale, quantity, base size, and whether the model is already assembled affect both timeline and quote.

Timeline realism

Rush deadlines can be reviewed, but early notice gives more room for prep, revisions, and finish quality.

Budget range

A rough range helps identify whether tabletop, showcase, or display-level work is the right fit for the project.

FAQ

Questions people usually have before commissioning

Can I send my own miniature?

Yes. Use the form to note whether the piece is already printed, assembled, or needs cleanup before paint.

Can this be print and paint together?

Yes. Select the print-and-paint option in the form so production and painting can be scoped together.

Do I need exact colors picked out?

No. A mood board, theme, or a few references is enough to start; exact callouts are optional.

How are quotes decided?

Quotes are based on model complexity, size, quantity, finish level, prep work, and how tight the deadline is.
